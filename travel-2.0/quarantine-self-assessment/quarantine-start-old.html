Assess your quarantine plan

Before travelling to Canada, you’re expected to make suitable plans for quarantine, within your own means. Non-Canadians who do not have an adequate plan may be denied entry into Canada.

Your plan will be evaluated by government representatives at the border to determine whether it is suitable.

Answer a few questions to find out if your quarantine plan is considered suitable.

Will you be able to get food and other basic necessities (such as water, medicine or heat) without leaving your place of quarantine? If you’re in an apartment or condo, your place of quarantine is your unit.

  • Yes, I will have food and basic necessities delivered to my door
  • No, I will not have access to necessities without leaving quarantine
Driving and flying to Canada have different quarantine rules

If you drive to Canada

You must go directly to your place of quarantine and stay for 14 days.You must have a suitable plan for your quarantine. Use these questions to find out if your plan is suitable.

If you fly to Canada

  1. On arrival to Canada and before leaving the airport, you will be required to take a COVID-19 test.
  2. Following the arrival test, you will need to go to your reserved hotel for 3 nights to await the test results.
  3. If you receive a negative result on your arrival test, you may be able to move on to your place of quarantine. You must have a suitable plan for your quarantine. Use these questions to find out if your plan is suitable.
