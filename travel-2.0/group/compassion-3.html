Canadians entering for compassionate reasons

DISCUSS REMOVE? If you are a a Canadian citizen, a person registered under the Indian Act, a permanent resident, or a protected person (refugee status), you are allowed to enter Canada.

We understand that you are arriving under difficult circumstances, but all travellers entering Canada, regardless of citizenship, must follow testing and quarantine requirements. The measures keep Canadians safe, particularly given the new COVID-19 variants in Canada and around the world. Follow the requirements checklist for your method of entry:

If you need to leave your place of quarantine for compassionate reasons

You need to request an exemption for a limited temporary release from mandatory quarantine to:

  • attend a funeral or end of life ceremony
  • be present during the final moments of life for a loved one
  • provide support or care to someone who's critically ill
  • provide care for a person who has a medical reason as to why they require support

You won't be permitted to leave quarantine without approval of your request and will be subject to fines and penalties for putting others at risk.

Foreign nationals applying for compassionate entry

All foreign nationals must apply for and receive written approval for compassionate entry to enter Canada. Without this advanced approval, you will likely be denied entry.

You can apply to enter for the following compassionate reasons:

  • to be present during the final moments of life for a loved one
  • to provide support to a person who is critically ill as determined by a licensed health care practitioner in Canada
  • to provide care to a person who has a medical reason as determined by a licensed health care practitioner in Canada
  • to attend a funeral or end of life ceremony

The person you will care for, visit at end of life, or attend the funeral of must reside in Canada and be either:

  • a Canadian citizen
  • a person registered under the Indian Act
  • a permanent resident of Canada
  • a temporary resident of Canada
  • a protected person (refugee status)

Caring for family

All foreign nationals must provide documentation from a licensed health care practitioner in Canada to request an exemption for compassionate reasons to enter Canada. However, you may be able to enter Canada to reunite with family members.

Request an exemption for compassionate reasons

1. Required documents for your application

Before you can begin your application, you must have the following documents completed and signed, depending on your situation.

Providing support or care for someone, or requesting to be present during the final moments of life

You require a Letter of Required Support.

  1. Download the Letter of Required Support Form (PDF).
  2. Send the Letter of required support to the licensed health care professional who can certify that your presence is required.

You need the completed and signed Letter of required support to submit your completed application to us for processing.

Each applicant requires their own letter of required support.

If you need to leave your place of quarantine for compassionate reasons during the 14-day period you need a Site Visit Authorization Form each time.

Leaving your place of quarantine for compassionate reasons

Attending a funeral or end of life ceremony

If you need to attend a funeral or end of life ceremony, you require proof of death. The following documents are examples that can be used as part of the death notification:

  • burial permit
  • death certificate
  • statement of death
  • medical certificate of death
  • letter from the funeral home

If you need to leave your place of quarantine for compassionate reasons during your quarantine period you need a Site Visit Authorization Form to attend.

Leaving your place of quarantine for compassionate reasons

Leaving your place of quarantine for compassionate reasons

You can request a limited release from quarantine for compassionate reasons as part of your compassionate entry application.

Private sites

You need to show you have signed permission from the site you're visiting as part of your application.

To be considered for limited release from quarantine a Site Visit Authorization Form is mandatory. You must get the signature of the person in charge of the location. This can be an administrator, proprietor or homeowner of the site.

Public outdoor sites

You don't need a Site Visit Authorization Form if you're visiting a public outdoor setting or site, for example, a funeral in a public park.

Provincial and territorial restrictions

Some provinces and territories may not allow for limited release from quarantine for compassionate reasons. This means that even if you receive approval from the Public Health Agency of Canada, a province or territory may have additional restrictions. In the event of conflicting requirements between federal restrictions and provincial or territorial travel restrictions, you must comply with those that are the most stringent.

Before applying, please check provincial or territorial requirements. Check back regularly as these restrictions can change.

Provincial and territorial travel restrictions

How to request authorization to visit a site

  1. Download the Site Visit Authorization Form (PDF).
  2. Send the Site Visit Authorization Form to the administrator, proprietor or homeowner of each site or location you need to visit during your limited release from quarantine, such as:
    • hospitals
    • funeral home
    • place of worship
    • retirement home
    • long-term care facility
    • palliative care facility, such as a hospice
    • private residence where the death or end of life ceremony is occurring or where you're supporting a critically ill person or caring for a person with a medical condition

You need the completed and signed Site Visit Authorization Form(s) to include with your application.

2. Complete your application

Don't start your application until you have the necessary completed and signed required documents.

Each traveller, including dependent children, must submit their own application form.

You won't be able to save the form for completion later. Applications will not be accepted with incomplete forms.

Apply for compassionate entry (Traveller Application)

To complete your application you must:

  1. Fill out all required fields on each page of the application.
    • there are 8 pages in total
    • clicking 'Next' to get to the following page doesn't submit information or your application
  2. Attach the required documents on page 6 of the application:
    • signed Letter of Required Support Form
    • proof of death (if applicable)
    • signed Site Authorization Form(s) (if applicable)
  3. Submit your application through the secure portal.
    • You'll receive an immediate message indicating, 'Submission completed successfully.'

You'll also receive a receipt email with an application number.

Your application will be assessed by the Public Health Agency of Canada. We'll confirm that your request meets the requirements for entry and limited release from mandatory quarantine for compassionate reasons.

Secure portal: Request an exemption for compassionate reasons

3. Once your application is approved

You must bring all required compassionate approvals with you to enter Canada. A government representative makes the final decision for entry at the port of entry based on the information presented to them at the time.
