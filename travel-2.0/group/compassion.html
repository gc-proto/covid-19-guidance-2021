Canadians entering for compassionate reasons

We understand that you are arriving under difficult circumstances, but all travellers entering Canada, regardless of citizenship, must follow testing and quarantine requirements. The measures keep Canadians safe, particularly given the new COVID-19 variants in Canada and around the world. Follow the requirements checklist for your method of entry:

  • Flying to Canada requirements checklist
  • Driving to Canada requirements checklist

You will need to complete a request for exemption online if:

You need to leave your place of quarantine for compassionate reasons

You may request an exemption for a limited temporary release from mandatory quarantine to:

  • attend a funeral or end of life ceremony
  • be present during the final moments of life for a loved one
  • provide support or care to someone who's critically ill
  • provide care for a person who has a medical reason as to why they require support

You won't be permitted to leave quarantine without approval of your request and will be subject to fines and penalties for putting others at risk.

Provincial and territorial restrictions

Before applying, please check provincial or territorial requirements.

Some provinces and territories may not allow release from quarantine for compassionate reasons. This means that even if you receive approval from the Public Health Agency of Canada, a province or territory may have additional restrictions. In the event of conflicting requirements between federal restrictions and provincial or territorial travel restrictions, you must comply with those that is are the most stringent.

Check back regularly as these restrictions can change.

You will quarantine where you will support, care or grieve

You need to request an exemption for compassionate reasons for release from mandatory quarantine if your quarantine location is also where:

  • you’re supporting a critically ill person
  • you’re caring for a person with a medical condition

Foreign nationals applying for compassionate entry

All foreign nationals must request an exemption online and receive written approval to enter Canada. Without this advanced approval, you will likely be denied entry.

You can apply to enter for the following compassionate reasons:

  • to be present during the final moments of life for a loved one
  • to provide support to a person who is critically ill as determined by a licensed health care practitioner in Canada
  • to provide care to a person who has a medical reason as determined by a licensed health care practitioner in Canada
  • to attend a funeral or end of life ceremony

The person you will care for, visit at end of life, or attend the funeral of must reside in Canada and be either:

  • a Canadian citizen
  • a person registered under the Indian Act
  • a permanent resident of Canada
  • a temporary resident of Canada
  • a protected person (refugee status)

Caring for family

All foreign nationals must provide documentation from a licensed health care practitioner in Canada to request an exemption for compassionate reasons.

However, you may be able to enter Canada to reunite with family members.

Once your application is approved

You must bring all required compassionate approvals with you to enter Canada. A government representative makes the final decision for entry at the port of entry based on the information presented to them at the time.
