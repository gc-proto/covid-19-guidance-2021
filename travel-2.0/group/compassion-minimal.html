Compassionate entry for travellers and limited release from quarantine

On this page

  • Canadians entering for compassionate reasons
  • Canadians returning after a compassionate visit abroad
  • Foreign nationals applying for compassionate entry and/or limited release from quarantine

Canadians entering for compassionate reasons

We understand that you are arriving under difficult circumstances, but all travellers entering Canada, regardless of citizenship, must follow testing and quarantine requirements. This is to keep everyone safe, particularly given the new COVID-19 variants in Canada and around the world.

Follow the requirements checklist for your method of entry:

  • Flying to Canada requirements checklist
  • Driving to Canada requirements checklist

You need to complete a request for exemption online if:

You need to leave your place of quarantine for compassionate reasons

You may request an exemption for a limited temporary release from mandatory quarantine to:

  • attend a funeral or end of life ceremony
  • be present during the final moments of life for a loved one
  • provide support or care to someone who's critically ill
  • provide care for a person who has a medical reason as to why they require support

You won't be allowed to leave quarantine without approval of your request. If you do, you'll be subject to fines and penalties for putting others at risk.

Provincial and territorial restrictions

Before applying, please check provincial or territorial requirements.

Some provinces and territories may not allow release from quarantine for compassionate reasons. This means that even if you receive approval from the Public Health Agency of Canada, a province or territory may have further travel restrictions you must follow.

Check back regularly as these restrictions can change.

You need to submit a complete request for a limited temporary release exemption online if your quarantine location is also where:

Canadians returning after a compassionate visit abroad

We understand that you left Canada under difficult circumstances. At this time, all travellers entering Canada must follow testing and quarantine requirements. This is to keep everyone safe, particularly given the new COVID-19 variants in Canada and around the world.

Follow the checklist for your method of entry:

  • Flying to Canada requirements checklist
  • Driving to Canada requirements checklist

If you're a resident of Canada flying home, you can now apply for an exemption from the mandatory hotel stopover.

We recommend that you submit your application for exemption before you leave Canada for compassionate reasons. Applications will not be processed upon arrival in Canada.

Requirements when you return to Canada

If you receive approval for a compassionate exemption from the mandatory hotel stopover, you must still follow all other requirements for air travellers when you return to Canada:

  • Flying to Canada requirements checklist

If you’re unable to meet these requirements, you may be directed to stay at a federal designated quarantine facility until you receive a negative result on your on-arrival COVID-19 test.

Foreign nationals applying for compassionate entry

All foreign nationals must request an exemption online and receive written approval to enter Canada. Without this advanced approval, you'll likely be denied entry.

You can apply for entry and/or limited release from quarantine for compassionate reasons if it's to:

  • be present during the final moments of life for a loved one
  • provide support to a person who is critically ill as determined by a licensed health care practitioner in Canada
  • provide care to a person who has a medical reason as determined by a licensed health care practitioner in Canada
  • attend a funeral or end of life ceremony

The person you'll care for, visit at end of life, or attend the funeral of must reside in Canada and be one of the following:

  • Canadian citizen
  • person registered under the Indian Act
  • permanent resident of Canada
  • temporary resident of Canada
  • protected person (refugee status)

If you don't qualify to enter for compassionate reasons

If you don't qualify to enter for compassionate reasons, you may still be able to enter Canada to reunite with family members.

Once your application is approved

Print or take screenshots of your compassionate approvals and attachments to bring with you to enter Canada.

A government official makes the final decision for entry at the border, based on the information presented to them at the time.

All international travellers must carry acceptable identification and a valid visa (if necessary) when entering Canada. Visit the Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada website to learn more about what travel documents are required.
